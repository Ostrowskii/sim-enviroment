Ecosystem Energy Balancing Notes
================================

Time model
----------
- 1 day = 1000 ticks
- 1 hour = 1000 / 24 = 41.67 ticks
- Decomposition delay for carcasses/rests = 4 hours = 166.67 ticks (~167)

Core energy equation (animals)
------------------------------
Per tick:

  total_cost = base_cost + (speed_magnitude * move_cost_factor) + (hunger * 0.0011)
  energy = energy - total_cost
  hunger = hunger + hunger_growth

Movement hierarchy (larger species pay more):
- insect move_cost_factor = 0.015
- fish move_cost_factor = 0.020
- duck move_cost_factor = 0.030
- leopard move_cost_factor = 0.045

Base metabolic hierarchy:
- insect base_cost = 0.028
- fish base_cost = 0.040
- duck base_cost = 0.055
- leopard base_cost = 0.085

Hysteresis (energy target behavior)
-----------------------------------
- Each animal has:
  - energyTarget (upper goal)
  - energyResume (lower trigger)
- duck and leopard use this strongly:
  - if energy >= energyTarget and hunger is low: rest/hibernate (minimal movement)
  - if energy <= energyResume: return to hunting/foraging

This prevents free hunting and reduces unnecessary movement drain.

Predation transfer model
------------------------
When predator eats prey:
- predator absorbs all prey.energy up to predator.maxEnergy
- overflow is dropped as leftover carcass at the kill position
- leftovers are edible by other species
- leftover decomposition starts after ~167 ticks, then decays slowly

Example:
- leopard energy = 250, leopard max = 280
- duck prey energy = 60
- absorbed = min(60, 30) = 30
- leftover = 30 (spawns as carcass/rest)

Target daily budgets (design intent)
------------------------------------
Duck:
- intended maintenance around ~90-120 energy/day depending movement + hunger state
- fish energy typically in the 35-55 range when hunted
- target: about 2 fish/day + occasional algae/scavenge keeps adults stable

Leopard:
- intended maintenance around ~140-180 energy/day depending movement + hunger state
- duck energy typically in the 75-120 range when hunted
- target: about 2 ducks/day is enough for adult survival

These are balancing targets, not hard guarantees. Real outcomes vary by seed and local population waves.

Birth energy cost
-----------------
- duck reproduction cost to parent = 10% of duck.maxEnergy
- leopard reproduction cost to parent = 10% of leopard.maxEnergy

This avoids "free births" and keeps predator reproduction tied to food surplus.

Producers and environmental compensation
----------------------------------------
To offset ecosystem energy dissipation from movement/metabolism:
- trees and algae receive stronger solar growth terms
- trees and algae consume local nutrients from a 2D nutrient grid
- each cell receives small baseline nutrient renewal each tick
- decomposition and waste return nutrients to local cells

Result:
- better long-run biomass support
- slower hunger pressure
- more chance to keep higher animal counts before collapses
